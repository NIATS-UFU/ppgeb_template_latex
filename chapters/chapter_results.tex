\section{Figures}

Figure \ref{fig:MyFigure} illustrates an example of a \LaTeX figure.

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[scale = 2]{LogoUFU.pdf}
\caption{\lipsum[1][1-3]}
\label{fig:MyFigure}
\end{figure}

It is possible to insert several images in one figure, each one with its own reference and label, as shown in Figure \ref{fig:MyFigure2}. In this case, you can mention Figure \ref{fig:SubFigure1} or Figure \ref{fig:SubFigure2}.

\begin{figure}[b]

\begin{subfigure}{0.5\textwidth}
\centering
\includegraphics[scale = 2]{LogoUFU} 
\caption{Caption1}
\label{fig:SubFigure1}
\end{subfigure}
\begin{subfigure}{0.5\textwidth}
\centering
\includegraphics[scale = 2]{LogoUFU}
\caption{Caption 2}
\label{fig:SubFigure2}
\end{subfigure}
\caption{Caption for this figure with two images.}
\label{fig:MyFigure2}
\end{figure}

 \bottomrule

\end{table}
